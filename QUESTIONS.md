## Q1 — MAIN WATER SHUTOFF
**Type:** Multiple Choice
**Topic:** Customer Safety Protocol

**Question:** Before you start any plumbing work in a customer's home, what is the FIRST thing you should do?

**Answers:**
A) Start working immediately — the customer called you so you have permission
B) **Put on shoe covers, introduce yourself, and locate the main water shutoff** ✓ CORRECT
C) Call Solomon to tell him you're at the job
D) Take a photo of the problem and post it to social media

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Always greet the customer professionally, protect their floors with shoe covers, and locate the main water shutoff before starting any work. This is basic safety and professionalism.

## Q2 — CUSTOMER COMMUNICATION (YES/NO + EXPLAIN)
**Type:** YES/NO + Text Explanation
**Topic:** Service Communication Standards

**Question:** Should a technician only tell the customer about the plumbing issue they originally called for?

**Format:**
- YES or NO radio buttons
- Text area for explanation

**Correct Answer:** NO

**Explanation:** Technicians should communicate ALL issues found during the visit, not just what the customer originally called for. This builds trust, prevents callbacks, and shows thoroughness. The customer deserves to know the full condition of their plumbing system.

**Example Good Explanation:** "No, because there might be other issues the customer doesn't know about. Finding everything helps them make informed decisions and prevents emergency calls later."

## Q14 — GAS VS ELECTRIC WATER HEATER DIAGNOSIS
**Type:** Multiple Choice
**Topic:** Water Heater Identification (Field Diagnostic)

**Question:** A customer calls about a water heater problem. You open the utility closet and see water on the floor. Which detail tells you this is a GAS water heater, not electric?

**Answers:**
A) The water is coming from a brass drain valve near the bottom — electric heaters only have plastic valves
B) The T&P relief valve on the side is leaking — gas heaters mount T&P on the side, electric mounts it on top
C) The water is hot to the touch — gas heaters run hotter and cause more condensation than electric
D) **The pilot light access window is fogged up from the leak — electric heaters don't have pilot access** ✓ CORRECT

**Correct Answer:** D

**Explanation:** Gas water heaters have a pilot light access window near the bottom for lighting/relighting the pilot. Electric heaters have wire connection panels instead. The fogged window is a dead giveaway.

**Key Differences:**
| Feature | Gas Water Heater | Electric Water Heater |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Pilot light | Yes (access window) | No |
| Vent pipe | Yes (top) | No |
| Gas line | Yes | No |
| Electrical | None or minimal | 240V connection |
| Burner chamber | Bottom | N/A |
